Packaging Design for Perfume Bottle

University Project

OVERVIEW

ROLE

I worked on a project for my university's graphic design class that involved redesigning the packaging for an item that held special meaning for me. I chose a bottle of oud perfume, a treasured father's present, and a memento from my time spent living in Dubai before relocating to Canada.

The goal of the redesign was to give the perfume's packaging a modern, eye-catching appearance while adhering to the traditional rose gold, silver, and gold color scheme of Arabia, which symbolizes the rich cultural legacy and sentimental connections connected to the fragrance.

The First Sketches

These were just initial sketches of brainstorming the ideas of a perfume bottle packaging with integrating a sense of royalty and brand into it.

The prototype

The images showcase the final prototype and layout of the package, where yellow symbolizes gold, a detail that was later hand-painted for added elegance. The design incorporates a unique opening mechanism, enhancing user experience, and features bilingual labeling in both English and Arabic, catering to a diverse audience as well as keeping in mind the roots of this Oud perfume.
