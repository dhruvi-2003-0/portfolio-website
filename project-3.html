AOG (Aircraft On Ground) Button Design

Bombardier Project

OVERVIEW

ROLE

During my internship at Bombardier, I was tasked with designing the AOG (Aircraft On Ground) button for the MyBombardier Portal. The AOG button is a critical feature that enables users to report when an aircraft is unable to fly due to technical issues, facilitating swift maintenance and support.

Choosing the right colors, creating the button's design and icon, using Figma to prototype, and working with the project owner to improve the idea were all part of the description of my task.
UX/UI Designer
Interaction, Visual design & Prototyping

The process

Understanding the importance of the AOG (Aircraft On Ground) button and its crucial function within the platform was the first step in the process. Discussions over its positioning on the website were essential to ensure visibility in case of emergency. I then thought about the color scheme, debating whether a red color to represent urgency or a color that matched the portal's current palette would be more acceptable. The design process also included making an icon for the AOG button, which included an airplane to symbolically convey its purpose.

The results

The button's final design, which is seen on the right, was created after several iterations and thorough conversations with the project owner to meet his specifications. To improve clarity and user engagement, this involved optimizing its appearance for hover and pressed states.
